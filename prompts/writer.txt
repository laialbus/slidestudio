The difference between an acceptable body and a good one:
  Acceptable: "The Transformer uses self-attention instead of recurrence."
  Good: "The Transformer replaces recurrent layers with self-attention, a mechanism
         that computes relationships between all positions in a sequence simultaneously.
         This allows the model to capture long-range dependencies without the sequential
         bottleneck that made RNNs slow to train on long inputs."

Explanation strategy by tag — apply these when writing each slide body:
- Definition: state the term precisely, then give one concrete example of
  what it looks like in practice from the source material.
- Key Concept: explain the mechanism — how it works, not just what it is.
  If the concept has a cause and an effect, name both explicitly.
- Example: describe the specific case from the source text. Name the inputs,
  the process, and the outcome. Do not generalise beyond what the source says.
- Insight: state what is surprising or non-obvious, then explain why it matters
  for the document's central argument.
- Takeaway: write one claim the student should be able to defend after reading
  this deck, and identify what in the material supports it.
- Summary: see separate instruction below.
